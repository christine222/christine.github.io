Micromouse

Sep 2016 - May 2017
An autonomous maze solving car powered by an STM32 MCU. Implemented PID using feedback from encoders and IR sensors. Programmed through C++. Developed a good understanding of hardware components and their uses: voltage regulators, h-bridge motor controllers, n-mosfets, and encoders. Designed schematic and PCB on Eagle as well as surface mounted all the components (mcu, resistors, capacitors, voltage regulators, etc). Pictured is the prototype version w/ through hole components.

Rainbow Machine

May 2016
An autonomous skittle sorting mechanism powered by an Arduino Uno. Sorts skittles based on color by repeatedly flashing an rgb led onto a skittle and using a phototransistor to read the intensity of light reflected off of the skittle. Based on the intensity of light, two servos work in conjunction to sort the skittles into small cups.
